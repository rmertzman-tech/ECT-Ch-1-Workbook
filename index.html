Who You Already Are: From Reaction to Responsibility

Reaction and Map Workbook · Testing the map inside a first reaction · Classroom Version 1.0.

Welcome.

This workbook helps you practice the first skill of the course: noticing a first reaction without treating it as the final truth.

A first reaction can matter. It may show that something feels unfair, harmful, disrespectful, confusing, or important. But a first reaction is not yet a tested judgment.

A first reaction deserves respect, but not automatic obedience.

Authorial Capability means the growing ability to notice, test, revise, and act responsibly from reasons you can stand behind.

At the end, this workbook will create a Workbook Output Card. You will paste that card into Question 1 of this week’s follow-up reflection.

2 Read the Case

Case Title: The Group Chat Silence

Maya is working on a class project with three other students. She sends an important message in the group chat asking whether everyone has finished their part.

One classmate, Jordan, reads the message but does not respond.

Maya immediately thinks:

“Jordan is lazy and does not care about the project.”

She feels angry and starts typing a harsh message.

Before she sends it, she pauses.

3 Ethics Move

This section asks what matters ethically.

Ethics begins when we ask what matters, who may be affected, and what kind of response could be responsible.

In this case, Maya’s reaction may point to something real. The group project matters. Shared work matters. Respect matters. Responsibility matters. But Maya still needs to test the reaction before turning it into a final judgment.

4 Critical-Thinking Move

This section asks how we can test whether we are seeing clearly enough.

A first view may reveal something important. It may also miss context, evidence, affected persons, conditions, or alternatives.

The point is not to erase the first view. The point is to test whether the first view is connected well enough to territory / reality before action.

Map: how the situation is showing up for someone.
Territory / reality: what is real, whether or not the first map fully shows it.

What part of Maya’s first reaction needs to be tested? Focus on the thought: “Jordan is lazy and does not care.”

What information, evidence, perspective, or context might still be missing? List at least two possibilities.

What is one other way the situation might be understood? Write a possible alternative interpretation that does not simply assume Jordan does not care.

6 Limit Check

Ethical thinking does not require pretending to know everything.

I know enough to take a responsible next step, but I still cannot conclude everything.

This is not weakness. It is part of responsible judgment.

What does Maya still not know? Write at least two things that cannot yet be concluded.

7 Reaction Test Step 6 — Choose a Responsible Next Step

Repair does not always mean everything can be fixed.

In Chapter 1, repair may mean revising a reaction, asking a better question, pausing before harm, protecting trust, or choosing a response that keeps responsibility possible.

Maya does not need to solve the whole group project immediately. She needs a next step she can stand behind.

Reaction Test Step 6: choose a responsible next step. The next step should fit what Maya knows, respect what she still does not know, and protect the possibility of repair.
